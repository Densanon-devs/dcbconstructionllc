Athol — Rural North Idaho & Mountain Views

Remodeling Craftsmanship for North Idaho Country Living

Athol sits along Highway 95 between Hayden and Sandpoint, surrounded by towering pines, mountain views, and the kind of wide-open spaces that draw people to North Idaho in the first place. This is a community where homeowners value independence, quality, and the rural lifestyle — and those values extend to how they want their homes built and maintained.

The homes in the Athol area reflect this independent spirit. You will find custom-built homes on multi-acre parcels, log cabins tucked into the trees, and everything from modest ranch-style houses along Highway 95 to larger properties near the Silverwood Theme Park corridor and the roads leading to Farragut State Park. Many of these homes were owner-built or constructed by small custom builders, which means each one has its own character — and its own set of remodeling considerations.

DCB Construction has worked on properties throughout the Athol area, from homes with well water systems and septic to properties with unique structural layouts. We bring the same level of craftsmanship to a bathroom remodel in a log home as we do to a full kitchen renovation in a modern custom build. And with Lake Pend Oreille and the surrounding mountains just up the road, we understand the climate demands — heavy snow loads, cold winters, and the temperature swings that can affect flooring, tile, and other materials over time.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — Athol

Do you charge extra for travel to Athol?

No. Athol falls within our standard service area along the Highway 95 corridor between Coeur d'Alene and Sandpoint. We do not add travel surcharges for properties in Athol or the surrounding area, including homes near Farragut State Park and the Silverwood corridor.

Can you remodel a bathroom in a log home?

Absolutely. We have experience working with log and timber-frame construction, which presents unique challenges for tile installation and moisture management. Log walls move and settle differently than stick-framed walls, so we use appropriate backer systems, flexible membranes, and installation techniques designed to accommodate that movement and prevent cracking or water intrusion.

What flooring works best for rural properties with heavy foot traffic?

For Athol-area homes where muddy boots, pets, and outdoor living are part of daily life, we typically recommend luxury vinyl plank or porcelain tile for high-traffic areas like entryways, mudrooms, and kitchens. Both materials are extremely durable, water-resistant, and easy to clean. For living areas, engineered hardwood offers warmth and beauty with better resistance to temperature and humidity changes than solid wood.

Do I need permits for a remodel on a rural Athol property?

Permit requirements in the Athol area depend on the scope of work and whether your property falls within city limits or unincorporated Kootenai County. Structural modifications, electrical work, and plumbing changes generally require permits regardless of location. We handle the permitting process for you and ensure all work is compliant with applicable codes.
