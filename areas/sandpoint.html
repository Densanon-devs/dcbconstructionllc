Sandpoint — Lake Pend Oreille & Mountains

Remodeling That Matches Sandpoint's Mountain and Lake Lifestyle

Sandpoint is one of those rare places where mountain and lake living come together. Nestled on the shores of Lake Pend Oreille with Schweitzer Mountain Resort just up the road, this Bonner County town attracts a mix of year-round residents, seasonal homeowners, and people who have turned their vacation property into a permanent address. It is a creative, outdoors-oriented community where homes are as varied as the people who live in them.

The housing in and around Sandpoint ranges widely. Downtown near Cedar Street Bridge and along the streets radiating from First Avenue, you will find older homes and cottages from the early to mid-1900s that carry tremendous charm but often need updated kitchens, bathrooms, and flooring. Along the lakeshore and up toward Schweitzer, there are larger custom homes and vacation properties where owners want high-end finishes — natural stone showers, heated tile floors, and custom cabinetry that reflect the mountain-lodge aesthetic.

DCB Construction serves Sandpoint homeowners with the same hands-on craftsmanship we bring to every project in Northern Idaho. We understand the building demands of this area — heavier snow loads, colder sustained temperatures, and the moisture considerations that come with lakefront living. Whether you are renovating a City Beach cottage or upgrading a ski chalet on Schweitzer, we deliver results that are built to last in Sandpoint's four-season climate.

Sandpoint homeowners have high expectations for their living spaces — and they should. Frequently Asked Questions — Sandpoint

Do you travel to Sandpoint from Coeur d'Alene for projects?

Yes. Sandpoint is within our regular service area, and we do not charge travel surcharges for projects in the Sandpoint and greater Bonner County area. We travel Highway 95 regularly and schedule our Sandpoint projects efficiently to ensure consistent daily progress without unnecessary downtime.

Can you work on my Sandpoint vacation home while I am out of town?

Absolutely. We regularly work with out-of-town homeowners on their Sandpoint vacation properties. We provide detailed written estimates and project plans upfront, and throughout the project we send daily photo updates and regular progress reports. We can coordinate with your property manager or keyholder for access, and we schedule a thorough walkthrough — in person or via video call — before closing out the project.

What tile or stone do you recommend for mountain homes near Schweitzer?

For homes in the Schweitzer Mountain area, we recommend porcelain tile or natural stone with low water absorption rates. These materials handle freeze-thaw conditions and temperature swings much better than standard ceramic. In entryways and mudrooms, textured porcelain tile provides excellent slip resistance for wet boots. For bathrooms, large-format porcelain with in-floor heating creates a warm, low-maintenance surface that stands up to heavy daily use.

Is remodeling a good investment for Sandpoint rental or vacation properties?

Sandpoint's vacation rental market is strong, driven by Lake Pend Oreille recreation in the summer and Schweitzer Mountain skiing in the winter. Updated kitchens, modern bathrooms, and quality flooring can significantly increase both your rental income and your property value. We can help you identify the most impactful improvements for your rental property and execute them efficiently to minimize downtime between bookings.
