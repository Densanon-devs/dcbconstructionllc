FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — Coeur d'Alene

Do I need a permit for a kitchen or bathroom remodel in Coeur d'Alene?

It depends on the scope of the work. In the City of Coeur d'Alene, structural changes, electrical work, and plumbing modifications typically require permits through the Kootenai County Building Department. We handle the permitting process for you so your project is fully compliant with local codes.

How long does a typical bathroom remodel take in CDA?

Most bathroom remodels in the Coeur d'Alene area take between two and four weeks, depending on the scope. A simple vanity and fixture swap can be completed in a week, while a full gut renovation with custom tile and in-floor heating may take closer to four weeks. We provide a detailed timeline before work begins.

Do you work on historic homes near downtown Coeur d'Alene?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience working on older homes in the Fort Grounds neighborhood, along Sherman Avenue, and in other established CDA neighborhoods. We understand the challenges that come with older construction, including lath-and-plaster walls, outdated plumbing, and unique framing. We approach every historic home with care and respect for its character.

What flooring do you recommend for lakefront homes in Coeur d'Alene?

For homes near Lake Coeur d'Alene, we often recommend luxury vinyl plank or porcelain tile. Both materials handle moisture and humidity well, are easy to maintain, and come in a wide range of styles. If you prefer the warmth of real hardwood, we can recommend engineered options that perform better in high-moisture environments than solid wood. We are happy to discuss the best options for your specific home during a free consultation.
